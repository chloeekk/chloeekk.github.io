Definition and Role of Sitemap

A sitemap is a file that you can use to provide information about the pages, videos or other files on your website, and also to illustrate the relationships between these contents.

The sitemap should include important metadata about each page, such as the date it was last updated, its priority, and how often it changes.

Search engines such as Google will read the sitemap to crawl the pages on your site more efficiently. The sitemap will tell search engines which pages and files on your site you think are important and will also provide important information about those files.

Here are some sitemap terms you may not familiar with:

  • sitemap priority: In a sitemap, page priority is a value between 0.0 and 1.0 that indicates the relative importance of a web page within the website hierarchy. A priority of 1.0 represents the most important page on the site, while a priority of 0.0 represents the least important page. The priority value provides a hint to search engines about which pages are most critical to a website’s structure and hierarchy, but it does not guarantee that those pages will be indexed or ranked higher.

  • sitemap changefreq: In a sitemap, changefreq stands for “change frequency” and indicates how often a web page is likely to change. The values that can be assigned to changefreq are limited to a few options: always, hourly, daily, weekly, monthly, yearly, or never. These values give search engines an idea of how frequently they should crawl the page to look for changes.

The changefreq value is useful for websites that have pages that change frequently, like news websites or online stores. In general, it’s a good practice to set changefreq to the most accurate value possible, but it’s not necessary to set this value for all pages on a website. Only include changefreq in your sitemap if you think it will be helpful for search engines to know the change frequency of a particular page.

Sitemap Examaples

Here are some examples of sitemaps:

XML Sitemap

This is the most commonly used type of sitemap, and it’s designed specifically for search engines. It lists all the pages of a website, including their URLs, last modification date, change frequency, and priority.

HTML Sitemap

This is a sitemap designed for human visitors to a website. It typically appears as a page on the website with a list of links to all the main pages on the site. HTML Sitemaps can be useful for helping visitors navigate the site and find the information they’re looking for.

Video Sitemap

This type of sitemap is used specifically for video content, and it provides information to Google about the video’s title, description, thumbnail URL, and other metadata.

Image Sitemap

This is a sitemap designed for images on a website. It provides metadata about each image, including its title, caption, location, and other details.

News Sitemap

This type of sitemap is used by websites that publish news articles. It provides information to Google about the publication date, title, and author of each article.

Sitemaps can help improve a website’s visibility and ranking in search engine results pages (SERPs) by providing search engines with important information about the website’s structure and hierarchy. By including a sitemap on your website, you can help ensure that search engines are able to crawl and index all the relevant pages on your site, which can ultimately lead to more traffic and higher rankings.

Do I Always Need to Use a Sitemap?

Not necessarily.

If the pages on your site are properly linked, i.e. your site is well structured and each page is accessible through a portal such as a navigation bar, then search engines will usually be able to find most of these pages. But even so, sitemaps help us to crawl larger, more complex sites or more specific documents more efficiently. If you don’t use a sitemap and the search engines don’t crawl your site, you’ll need to manually submit the page addresses to be indexed by the search engines.

You may need a site map if:

  • Your site is large. In general, on large sites, it can be more difficult to ensure that every page on the site is linked to by at least one other page. As a result, search engine crawlers are more likely to not find some of your new pages.

  • Your site is new and does not have many external links to it. Googlebot and other web crawlers crawl web pages by following links between pages. Therefore, if there are no other sites linking to your page, search engine crawlers may not find your page.

How do I add a Shopify Sitemap?

Shopify automatically generates an XML sitemap for every store, which includes all the links to the products, pages, collections, and blog posts on the website. This sitemap is designed to help search engines crawl and index your store’s pages more efficiently, resulting in better search engine visibility and rankings.

Shopify’s sitemap also includes additional features such as image metadata, allowing search engines to index images on your website more thoroughly. Shopify also automatically updates the sitemap whenever new products, pages, or collections are added or edited, ensuring that search engines always have the most up-to-date information about your store.

In addition to the automatically generated sitemap, Shopify also allows you to customize your sitemap by excluding certain pages or collections from the sitemap or changing the priority or frequency of certain pages. This feature can be particularly useful for larger stores with a lot of content, as it allows you to prioritize the most important pages for search engines to crawl and index.

Some Free Sitemap Generator

There several free sitemap generator tools available that can help you create a sitemap for your website. Here are a few examples:

XML Sitemap Generator

This is a free online tool that allows you to generate an XML sitemap for your website by simply entering your website URL. It can handle sites with up to 500 pages and offers additional features like priority and last modification date.

Screaming Frog SEO Spider

This is a desktop application that crawls websites to create a detailed list of all the pages on the site, including broken links, page titles, and meta descriptions. It offers a free version that can crawl up to 500 URLs on your website.

Yoast SEO Plugin

If you’re using WordPress, the Yoast SEO plugin is a popular choice for generating a sitemap. The plugin automatically creates an XML sitemap for your website and allows you to customize settings like priority and frequency.

GSiteCrawler

This is a free sitemap generator tool that can create an XML sitemap for any website. It also offers features like custom filters and URL exclusions to help you generate the most accurate sitemap possible.

These free sitemap generator tools can be a great way to create a sitemap for your website quickly and easily. However, it’s important to note that they may have limitations in terms of the number of pages they can crawl or the level of customization they offer. If you have a larger website or specific requirements for your sitemap, you may need to consider a paid sitemap generator tool or work with a developer to create a custom solution.

Sitemap Checker

A sitemap checker is a tool that analyzes an XML sitemap to identify any errors or issues that may be preventing search engines from crawling and indexing your website correctly. Here are a few examples of sitemap checker tools:

Google Search Console

This is a free tool provided by Google that allows you to check your sitemap for errors and view statistics on how your site is performing in search results.

Screaming Frog SEO Spider

In addition to generating sitemaps, Screaming Frog also offers a sitemap checker tool that analyzes sitemaps for errors and lists out any issues it finds, such as broken links or pages with incorrect metadata.

Sitemap Generator

This is a free online tool that not only generates sitemaps but also checks them for errors, including dead links, incorrect syntax, and missing tags.

XML Sitemap Validator

This is a free online tool that checks sitemaps for errors and provides a detailed report on any issues it finds, including duplicates, incorrect URLs, and invalid characters.

Using a sitemap checker tool can help ensure that your sitemap is correctly formatted and optimized for search engines, which can improve your website’s visibility and ranking in search results. Regularly checking your sitemap for errors can also help you identify any issues with your website’s structure or content that may be negatively impacting your SEO.

URL Extractor from Sitemap

A URL extractor from sitemap is a tool that extracts all the URLs present in an XML sitemap and lists them out in a user-friendly format. This can be useful for a variety of purposes, such as checking if all the pages on your website are included in the sitemap or analyzing the structure of your website’s hierarchy.

There are several online tools available that allow you to extract URLs from sitemaps quickly and easily. Here are a few examples:

XML Sitemap Generator

This is a free online tool that not only generates sitemaps but also allows you to extract URLs from an existing sitemap. Simply enter the sitemap URL, and the tool will list out all the URLs present in the sitemap.

Sitemap Writer Pro

This is a desktop application that allows you to generate and edit XML sitemaps. It also includes a URL extractor feature that allows you to extract all the URLs from an existing sitemap and save the results in a CSV file.

Screaming Frog SEO Spider

In addition to generating sitemaps, Screaming Frog also offers a URL extractor feature that allows you to extract all the URLs from an existing sitemap and export them in a CSV file.

Using a URL extractor from sitemap tool can make it easier to analyze the structure and content of your website by providing a comprehensive list of all the pages on your site in one place. This can help you identify any issues with your website’s hierarchy or content that may be negatively impacting your SEO or user experience.
