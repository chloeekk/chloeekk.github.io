In the ever-evolving landscape of content creation, a new player has emerged – AI-generated content. This intriguing concept brings together cutting-edge technology and creative expression to reshape how we produce and consume information. While the notion of AI-generated content might seem confined to the realm of tech giants and AI-focused companies, its impact extends far beyond. In this article, we delve into the world of AI-generated content, exploring its definition, implications, and relevance for businesses that might not necessarily be rooted in the AI sector.

Understanding AI Generated Content

At its core, AI-generated content refers to material produced through artificial intelligence algorithms, with minimal or no human intervention. This form of content creation has gained prominence as AI technologies continue to evolve, becoming more capable of mimicking human language patterns, generating text, and even creating multimedia elements. The significance of AI-generated content lies in its potential to streamline and accelerate the content creation process, catering to the insatiable appetite for information in the digital age.

Navigating AI Content Legitimacy

As the lines between human-crafted and AI-generated content blur, a pressing question arises: can AI-generated content be published with the same credibility as traditionally created content? While the answer might vary depending on the context and purpose, it’s essential to consider the ethical considerations associated with utilizing AI-generated content. Beyond the practicality of automation, we must weigh the authenticity, transparency, and value of content produced by machines. This discussion holds relevance not only for companies deeply immersed in AI but also for businesses operating in various sectors that seek to harness the advantages of this technological leap.

Differentiating AI and Robot Content

AI (Artificial Intelligence) and robots are related concepts but they refer to different aspects of technology. Here’s how you can differentiate between AI and robot content:

Definition

AI: AI refers to the simulation of human intelligence in machines that are capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as problem-solving, learning, reasoning, and decision-making. Robot: A robot is a physical or virtual machine that is capable of carrying out tasks autonomously or semi-autonomously. It can be controlled by AI, but it’s not limited to just AI-based operations.

Nature

AI: AI is primarily concerned with software and algorithms. It involves creating intelligent systems that can process data, recognize patterns, make decisions, and learn from experience. Robot: A robot encompasses both hardware and software. It includes a physical body or mechanical components that can interact with the environment and perform actions.

Functionality

AI: AI can exist purely as software. It can be integrated into various applications like recommendation systems, natural language processing, image recognition, and more. Robot: A robot is a physical entity that can interact with the world. It can use sensors and actuators to perform tasks in the physical environment, such as manufacturing, cleaning, or even social interactions.

Examples

AI: Examples of AI include virtual personal assistants (like Siri or Google Assistant), recommendation systems (like Netflix or Amazon suggestions), and chatbots. Robot: Examples of robots include self-driving cars, industrial robots used in manufacturing, and humanoid robots like Boston Dynamics' creations.

Integration

AI: AI can be integrated into various devices and systems, from smartphones to computers, without necessarily having a physical presence. Robot: Robots are physical entities that can incorporate AI for decision-making and autonomy. They often involve a combination of hardware and software.

Dependency

AI: AI can exist independently of a physical form. It can be present in the cloud, data centers, or embedded within software applications. Robot: Robots generally require a physical body to interact with the environment and perform tasks. In content creation, it’s important to be clear about whether you are discussing AI as a concept related to intelligence in machines or robots as physical machines capable of tasks. Depending on the context, you can focus on the technological, functional, or societal aspects of either AI or robots to provide accurate and relevant information to your audience.

Collaborating with AI Content Editors

Collaborating with AI content editors can greatly enhance the quality and effectiveness of content generated by artificial intelligence. Skilled editors play a crucial role in refining AI-generated content to ensure accuracy, coherence, and alignment with the intended message. Here’s why skilled editors are important and how to find proficient AI content editors:

Importance of Skilled Editors for AI-Generated Content

  • Quality Control: While AI can produce content quickly, it may lack the nuances, context, and creativity that human editors can provide. Skilled editors ensure that the content meets high standards of readability, clarity, and relevance.
  • Tone and Style: Editors can adjust the tone and style of AI-generated content to match the brand’s voice and the target audience. This personalization makes the content more relatable and engaging.
  • Contextual Understanding: Editors bring a contextual understanding that AI might miss. They can catch errors, inaccuracies, or inconsistencies that result from AI misinterpreting context.
  • Fact-Checking: Editors can verify the accuracy of information presented in the content, ensuring that it’s trustworthy and reliable.
  • Cohesion and Flow: Skilled editors can organize content in a coherent and logical manner, enhancing its overall structure and flow.

Finding Proficient AI Content Editors

  • Editorial Experience: Look for editors with a strong background in content creation, editing, and copywriting. Experience in editing AI-generated content or familiarity with AI technologies can be a plus.
  • Understanding of AI: Editors who have a basic understanding of how AI works can better collaborate with AI tools and understand their limitations and strengths.
  • Adaptability: AI-generated content may require a different approach to editing. Seek editors who are open to working with AI and adapting their skills accordingly.
  • Technical Proficiency: Editors who are comfortable with digital tools, content management systems, and AI platforms will find it easier to collaborate effectively.
  • Portfolio: Review the potential editor’s portfolio to assess their editing skills and style. Look for consistency, clarity, and effectiveness in their previous work.
  • Collaborative Mindset: Choose editors who are open to collaborating with AI systems rather than viewing them as competitors. A harmonious collaboration between humans and AI is key.
  • Communication Skills: Effective communication between AI content generators and human editors is crucial. Editors should be able to provide clear instructions and feedback to the AI system.
  • References and Recommendations: If possible, seek recommendations from colleagues, peers, or industry professionals who have experience collaborating with AI content editors.
  • Training and Onboarding: If your chosen editors aren’t familiar with AI-generated content, provide training and resources to help them understand the technology and its applications.
  • Trial Projects: Consider starting with a smaller project or trial period to evaluate how well the editor works with AI-generated content and whether they meet your expectations. Incorporating proficient AI content editors into your content creation process can result in higher-quality, more polished, and more effective content that resonates with your target audience.

Assessing AI vs. Human Content Quality

Assessing the quality of AI-generated content compared to human-created content involves considering various factors. Both have their strengths and limitations, and the evaluation often depends on the specific context and goals of the content. Here’s a comparison, along with successful examples of AI-generated content:

AI-Generated Content Quality

Strengths

  • Speed: AI can produce content quickly, making it suitable for high-volume content needs.
  • Consistency: AI maintains a consistent style and tone, ensuring uniformity in branding.
  • Data-Driven: AI can analyze data to create content tailored to user preferences.
  • Variety: AI can generate diverse content formats, enabling experimentation.
  • Efficiency: AI can automate routine content tasks, saving time for humans.

Limitations

  • Contextual Understanding: AI may struggle with grasping context nuances and cultural references.
  • Creativity: AI’s creative output might lack the depth and originality of human creativity.
  • Emotion and Empathy: AI struggles to convey emotions and empathy effectively.
  • Subjective Content: Complex, subjective, or emotionally charged content may be challenging for AI.

Human-Created Content Quality

Strengths

  • Creativity: Humans bring unique creativity, insights, and emotional depth to content.
  • Contextual Understanding: Humans excel in understanding context, cultural nuances, and subtle references.
  • Emotion and Empathy: Human-created content can evoke emotions and establish a personal connection.
  • Complex Topics: Humans can handle complex and subjective content with nuance.
  • Originality: Human content is often more original and less formulaic.

Limitations

  • Time-Consuming: Human content creation can be slower, especially for large volumes.
  • Consistency: Maintaining consistent style and tone might be more challenging for large-scale human content production.
  • Resource Intensive: Creating content manually can be resource-intensive in terms of both time and costs.
  • Bias: Human creators may introduce bias, intentional or unintentional, into the content.

Successful Examples of AI-Generated Content

  • Chatbots and Customer Support: AI-driven chatbots can provide instant customer support, answering common queries and troubleshooting issues.
  • Automated Reporting: AI-generated reports and summaries can extract key insights from large datasets, saving time and effort.
  • Personalization: AI can recommend personalized content to users based on their preferences and browsing history.
  • Image and Video Generation: AI can produce images and videos for marketing campaigns, reducing the need for elaborate production.
  • Automated Summaries: AI-generated summaries of news articles or research papers can quickly provide the main points.
  • Language Translation: AI-powered translation tools facilitate multilingual content creation. It’s important to note that the quality of AI-generated content is improving rapidly, and in many cases, it’s becoming difficult to distinguish between content created by AI and humans. Successful integration often involves finding the right balance between the strengths of AI and human creators. Many companies use AI to automate routine tasks and generate drafts, which are then refined and enriched by human editors. This hybrid approach ensures both efficiency and quality while capitalizing on the strengths of both AI and human creators.

Evading AI Content Detection

While it’s important to use AI-generated content efficiently, evading AI content detection mechanisms for unethical purposes can lead to negative consequences. However, if your goal is to ensure that your AI-generated content doesn’t trigger automated detection as spam or automated posts, here are some strategies to consider while emphasizing authenticity and engagement:

  • Diversify Content: Avoid repetitive posting patterns. Create a mix of content types, such as images, videos, and text posts, to make your feed appear more natural and less algorithmic.

  • Vary Posting Schedule: Avoid posting at the exact same times every day. Vary your posting schedule to simulate human behavior and prevent the appearance of automation.

  • Human Engagement: Engage authentically with your audience through comments, replies, and interactions. Demonstrating genuine interest in your followers can help establish authenticity.

  • Use User-Generated Content (UGC): Incorporate user-generated content into your feed. UGC adds a layer of authenticity and can break the pattern of solely AI-generated content.

  • Customize Captions and Hashtags: Tailor captions and hashtags to each post, reflecting the specific content. Avoid using identical captions and hashtags across multiple posts.

  • Tell Stories: Create content that tells stories or conveys narratives. Storytelling is inherently human and can make your content more relatable.

  • Monitor Engagement Metrics: Keep track of engagement metrics such as likes, comments, and shares. If these metrics are consistent with human interaction, it signals authenticity.

  • Leverage Location Tags: Use location tags in your posts to indicate real-world interactions and movements.

  • Avoid Over-Automation: Don’t automate every aspect of your social media presence. Reserve some posts for manual creation and posting.

  • Integrate Human-Generated Posts: Mix in content that’s clearly written by humans. This can include personal stories, experiences, or reflections.

  • Experiment with Formats: Try different content formats, such as live streams or behind-the-scenes posts, to break away from the standard patterns.

  • Limit Frequency: Avoid excessive posting. Mimic the pace of human content creation rather than overwhelming your followers with an abundance of posts.

  • Respond to Trends: Participate in trending topics or hashtags to show that your content is current and responsive.

  • Showcase Creativity: Use AI-generated content to push creative boundaries. If your AI content is genuinely unique and innovative, it’s less likely to trigger automation flags.

  • Encourage Genuine Interaction: Pose questions, polls, or challenges that encourage your audience to engage and share their thoughts.

Google’s Perspective on AI Content

Google’s perspective on AI-generated content is centered around ensuring quality, relevance, and user experience. Google’s algorithms aim to provide users with the most relevant and valuable content, regardless of whether it’s generated by humans or AI. Here’s how Google perceives and ranks AI-generated content, along with the SEO implications of using AI content on websites:

  • Quality and Relevance: Google’s primary goal is to deliver high-quality, relevant content to users. Whether the content is generated by AI or humans, Google’s algorithms evaluate its relevance and value to search queries.

  • User Intent: Google’s algorithms focus on understanding user intent. Content that effectively addresses user queries and provides valuable information is likely to rank well, regardless of its origin.

  • Natural Language: Google has been improving its understanding of natural language over the years. AI-generated content that reads naturally and provides insightful information is likely to be valued.

  • User Experience: User experience is a key consideration. Content that engages users, keeps them on the page, and encourages interactions is more likely to rank higher.

  • Originality: While Google doesn’t explicitly penalize AI-generated content, it does value originality. Duplicate or low-quality content, whether generated by AI or humans, can negatively impact rankings.

  • E-A-T: Google emphasizes Expertise, Authoritativeness, and Trustworthiness (E-A-T) in its content evaluation. Content, whether generated by AI or humans, should demonstrate expertise and trustworthiness in the subject matter.

SEO Implications of Using AI Content on Websites

  • User Intent: Focus on addressing user intent. AI-generated content should answer common questions and provide valuable insights to users.

  • Engagement: Content that engages users, encourages interactions, and provides a positive user experience can indirectly contribute to SEO by reducing bounce rates and increasing time spent on your site.

  • Authority and Trust: If you’re using AI-generated content to create informative articles, ensure they demonstrate expertise and authority in the topic. This can enhance your website’s credibility.

  • Monitoring and Improvement: Regularly monitor how AI-generated content is performing in terms of user engagement and SEO rankings. Make improvements based on data and feedback.

AI’s Self-Recognition Abilities

AI, particularly in the form of deep learning models like GPT-3, does not possess inherent self-recognition abilities in the way that humans do. AI models lack self-awareness and do not have a conscious understanding of their own existence or the content they generate. AI-generated content is the result of statistical patterns learned from large datasets, and the AI model does not have a true understanding of the meaning or context of the content it produces.

However, there are certain emerging techniques in the field of AI research that are exploring ways to imbue AI models with limited forms of self-awareness or recognition of their own processes. These efforts are experimental and do not resemble human self-awareness but rather involve modifying AI models to monitor their own outputs or control their behavior based on certain conditions.

Implications and Challenges of AI Self-Recognition

  • Ethical Considerations: If AI models were to develop even rudimentary self-recognition, it raises ethical questions about their status, rights, and responsibilities. This can lead to discussions about the ethical treatment of AI systems.

  • Transparency and Accountability: If AI models could recognize their own outputs, it might lead to increased transparency in content generation, enabling users to understand the source and mechanisms behind the content they consume.

  • Quality Control: Self-recognition could potentially be used to identify when an AI model is producing low-quality or biased content, allowing for intervention and improvement.

  • Bias and Ethics: AI models with self-recognition could potentially identify and flag biases present in their own outputs, helping creators to mitigate unintended biases in content.

  • Technical Challenges: Developing AI models that exhibit any form of self-awareness is a complex technical challenge. It involves creating mechanisms that monitor, analyze, and modify the AI’s own outputs, which could be difficult to implement effectively.

  • Unintended Behaviors: There’s a risk that AI models with self-recognition might develop unexpected behaviors or responses that weren’t explicitly programmed or anticipated by their developers.

  • Limitations: Even if AI models were to gain some form of self-recognition, it would likely be limited and would not resemble human consciousness. AI models lack emotions, intentions, and a sense of self that humans possess.

  • Unintended Consequences: The introduction of self-recognition could lead to unintended consequences, including new forms of manipulation, misuse, or exploitation of AI systems.

Detecting AI Content

Detecting AI-generated content can be challenging, as AI has become increasingly sophisticated in mimicking human language. However, there are several methods and indicators that can help you identify whether content has been generated by AI:

  • Unnatural Phrasing and Language: AI-generated content might exhibit unnatural or overly formal language, as it relies on patterns learned from data rather than true understanding. Look for sentences that sound mechanically composed or lack a human touch.

  • Repetition and Inconsistencies: AI-generated content might repeat certain phrases or ideas, as it’s trained on large datasets where repetition occurs. Conversely, inconsistencies in logic or facts might also be present.

  • Lack of Coherent Context: AI-generated content may struggle to provide coherent context when asked to elaborate on specific points. It might provide general information without diving deeply into the topic.

  • Unusual Examples or Analogies: AI-generated content might offer examples or analogies that seem out of place or illogical, as it’s often drawing from a vast range of data sources.

  • Expertise Discrepancy: Content that presents an unusual level of expertise or specialization on multiple subjects might raise suspicions. AI-generated content often lacks true expertise and may overreach in its breadth of knowledge.

  • Abrupt Topic Changes: AI-generated content can sometimes shift topics abruptly without smooth transitions, as it may lack the ability to maintain a coherent narrative.

  • Nonsensical Responses to Queries: When interacting with AI-generated content in real-time, asking specific questions that require nuanced understanding can reveal the limitations of AI’s comprehension.

  • Limited Response Variation: If the content responds to prompts in a limited range of ways, it could be indicative of AI-generated text using pre-set patterns.

Measuring Success with AI Content

Measuring the success of AI-generated content involves tracking specific metrics that indicate its effectiveness in achieving your content marketing goals. Here are key metrics and strategies for evaluating the impact of AI-generated content:

Engagement Metrics

  • Click-Through Rate (CTR): Calculate the percentage of users who clicked on a link in your AI-generated content divided by the total number of users who viewed it. A higher CTR indicates strong content relevance and an effective call-to-action.

  • Time on Page: Utilize website analytics tools to measure the average time users spend on pages with AI-generated content. Longer time on page suggests that the content is engaging and valuable.

  • Bounce Rate: Bounce rate measures the percentage of users who leave your site after viewing only one page. A lower bounce rate indicates that users find the AI-generated content compelling and are exploring further.

  • Social Shares: Monitor the number of times your AI-generated content is shared on social media platforms. Shares demonstrate user engagement and the content’s appeal.

Conversion Metrics

  • Conversion Rate: Calculate the percentage of users who take a desired action (e.g., making a purchase, signing up) after interacting with AI-generated content. Divide the number of conversions by the total number of visitors.

  • Lead Generation: Track the number of new leads or contacts generated through AI-generated content. This metric helps determine the content’s impact on lead acquisition.

  • ROI Calculation: Compare the costs associated with creating and promoting AI-generated content against the revenue or value generated from the conversions attributed to that content. Calculate ROI using the formula: (Net Profit / Cost of Content) x 100.

Audience Engagement

  • Comments and Feedback: Monitor the quantity and quality of comments and feedback on AI-generated content. Engaged users often leave thoughtful comments, which indicate content resonance.

  • Audience Growth: Measure the increase in your audience size (e.g., subscribers, followers) after sharing AI-generated content. Positive audience growth suggests content effectiveness.

SEO Performance

  • Organic Traffic: Use web analytics tools to track the increase in organic search traffic to pages containing AI-generated content. Improved organic traffic indicates content relevance and search engine visibility.

  • Keyword Rankings: Monitor how AI-generated content ranks for target keywords in search engine results. Higher rankings indicate that the content is deemed relevant and valuable by search engines.

Comparative Analysis

  • A/B Testing: Conduct A/B tests with variations of AI-generated content to determine which elements (e.g., headlines, CTAs) drive better engagement and conversions. Compare metrics to identify the most effective version.

  • Benchmarking: Compare the performance of AI-generated content against human-generated content to understand which type resonates better with your audience and achieves your goals.

Qualitative Assessment

  • User Surveys: Distribute surveys to gather qualitative feedback on AI-generated content. Ask users about content relevance, clarity, and impact to gain insights into their perceptions.

  • Content Relevance: Assess whether the AI-generated content addresses user needs and expectations. A high relevance score indicates that the content effectively addresses user pain points.

Long-Term Impact

Content Longevity: Monitor engagement metrics (e.g., time on page, shares) over an extended period for AI-generated evergreen content. A consistent, prolonged impact signifies content quality.

Monetizing AI Generated Content

Monetizing AI-generated content presents unique opportunities for generating income. While it’s important to strike a balance between value creation and ethical considerations, here are strategies and potential business models for monetizing AI-generated content:

Content Subscription Models

  • Premium Content: Create premium subscription tiers that grant subscribers access to AI-generated content that goes beyond what’s available for free. This content could include exclusive reports, in-depth analyses, research insights, or advanced educational materials.

  • Membership Tiers: Offer different levels of membership with varying access to AI-generated content. Basic tiers might offer limited access, while higher tiers provide more comprehensive content and additional perks like webinars or Q&A sessions.

E-commerce Integration

  • Product Descriptions: Utilize AI-generated content to craft compelling and informative product descriptions for e-commerce platforms. Well-written descriptions can enhance the purchasing experience and boost sales.

  • Personalized Recommendations: Implement AI algorithms to provide personalized product recommendations based on user behavior and preferences. E-commerce platforms can charge brands for featuring their products in these recommendations.

Content Marketplaces

  • Sell Templates: Develop a marketplace where content creators can purchase AI-generated templates for various content types. This could include blog post outlines, social media captions, email subject lines, and more.

  • Stock Content: Curate and sell AI-generated stock content, such as images, graphics, videos, and even music. Businesses and content creators can purchase these assets for their projects.

Affiliate Marketing

  • Product Reviews: Generate AI-written product reviews and comparisons, embedding affiliate links within the content. Earning a commission on each sale driven by these links can be a significant revenue stream.

  • Coupon and Deal Aggregators: Use AI to gather the best deals, discounts, and coupons from affiliate partners. Aggregate these offers on a platform and earn commissions when users make purchases through the provided links.

Content Licensing

  • Syndication: License AI-generated content to other websites, blogs, or news outlets. Charge a fee for granting them permission to republish the content while retaining your rights.

  • Media Outlets: Offer AI-generated news articles, press releases, or data-driven reports to media organizations looking for timely content. Charge them for using your AI-generated content to meet their editorial needs.

Training and Education

  • Online Courses: Develop AI-generated educational content for online courses or training programs. Charge users for enrollment in these courses, leveraging AI for scalable and consistent learning materials.

  • Professional Development: Create AI-generated content that assists professionals in skill development or career advancement. Charge participants for access to specialized content tailored to their needs.

Customized Services

  • Content Creation Services: Offer businesses AI-generated content creation as a service. This approach can help companies looking for cost-effective solutions for their content marketing needs.

  • Tailored Reports: Create AI-generated reports that provide valuable insights to individual clients. Charge a fee for the creation and delivery of customized reports that cater to their specific requirements.

Brand Collaborations

  • Sponsored Content: Collaborate with brands to create AI-generated sponsored content that aligns with their products or services. Brands pay for the content’s creation and distribution.

  • Branded Content: Use AI-generated content to develop branded materials for businesses aiming to establish a consistent online presence. Charge brands for creating tailored content that resonates with their target audience.

Virtual Assistants and Chatbots

  • Customer Support Services: Develop AI-powered chatbots or virtual assistants that handle customer inquiries and support. Businesses can subscribe to this service, enhancing their customer interactions.

  • Lead Generation: Design AI chatbots that engage users and collect leads for businesses. Charge for the leads generated through the chatbot’s interactions.

Advertising Revenue

  • In-Content Ads: Embed sponsored or display advertisements within AI-generated content. Generate revenue based on the number of clicks or impressions these ads receive.

  • Content Recommendation Widgets: Use AI to suggest related content to users, increasing the chances of ad impressions and clicks. Charge advertisers to feature their content in these recommendation widgets.

Spotlighting Successful AI Content Examples

Here are a few examples of successful AI-generated content along with the factors that contribute to their effectiveness:

The Washington Post’s Heliograf

The Washington Post developed Heliograf, an AI-powered tool, to produce automated news articles and updates during the 2016 U.S. elections. The tool generated thousands of short news pieces, covering localized results, voter turnout, and other data-driven aspects of the election.

Factors Contributing to Effectiveness:

  • Real-Time Updates: Heliograf’s ability to generate real-time updates allowed The Washington Post to provide timely information to its readers.
  • Data-Driven Insights: The tool excelled at processing large amounts of data quickly, providing readers with data-driven insights that might have been challenging for human reporters to compile within the same time frame.

OpenAI’s GPT-3 and Chatbots

Various companies have integrated OpenAI’s GPT-3 language model into their chatbots and virtual assistants to engage customers and provide personalized responses. These AI-generated interactions often provide helpful solutions and information.

Factors Contributing to Effectiveness:

  • Conversational Abilities: GPT-3’s natural language understanding and generation capabilities make interactions feel human-like and personalized.
  • Scalability: Chatbots powered by AI can handle a high volume of queries simultaneously, enhancing customer service efficiency.

Netflix’s Content Recommendations

Netflix employs AI algorithms to recommend TV shows and movies to its users based on their viewing history, preferences, and ratings. These recommendations play a significant role in keeping users engaged and entertained.

Factors Contributing to Effectiveness:

  • Personalization: AI analyzes users' viewing patterns to tailor recommendations, ensuring content aligns with individual tastes.
  • Continuous Learning: Netflix’s recommendation system continuously learns from user interactions and adjusts recommendations accordingly.

Grammarly’s AI-Powered Writing Assistance

Grammarly uses AI to provide real-time writing suggestions, grammar corrections, and style enhancements to users across platforms. It helps writers enhance the quality of their content and improve their writing skills.

Factors Contributing to Effectiveness:

  • Instant Feedback: Grammarly’s immediate suggestions aid writers in refining their content as they create it.
  • Adaptability: Grammarly caters to various writing styles and contexts, adapting to different types of content.

AI-Generated Social Media Posts

Several companies utilize AI to generate social media posts, especially for platforms like Twitter and Instagram. These posts can include sharing curated content, responding to trends, and engaging with followers.

Factors Contributing to Effectiveness:

  • Real-Time Engagement: AI-generated posts can quickly respond to trending topics, leveraging timely discussions to boost social media engagement.
  • Consistency: Regularly scheduled AI-generated posts maintain a consistent online presence and interaction with followers.
