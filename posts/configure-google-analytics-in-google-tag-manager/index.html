Configure Google Analytics 4 in Google Tag Manager

Google Tag Manager simplifies the process of configuring and overseeing GA4 events within a single platform. To transmit event information from Google Tag Manager to GA4, you must employ a pair of complementary tags:

  • Google Tag The Google Tag facilitates the transfer of data from your website to Google Analytics and any other designated destinations. For each domain, generate a unique Google Tag and embed it on all the websites you wish to track.

  • Google Analytics: GA4 Event The GA4 Event tag empowers you to establish website events without the necessity of coding. The event data subsequently appears within your GA4 reports. Feel free to generate as many event tags as required to monitor your recommended or customized events effectively.

Before you begin

This guide takes into consideration that you have completed the following steps:

  1. Established a Google Analytics 4 account and property.
  2. Created a web data stream for your website.
  3. Configured and implemented Tag Manager.

Furthermore, it presumes that you possess the following:

  1. Access to the Tag Manager container associated with the website.
  2. A role of Editor or higher in the Google Analytics account.

Step 1: Create a Google tag

To initiate the process, begin by establishing a Google tag to transmit data to your Analytics property.

  1. Launch Google Tag Manager.
  2. Choose the container you wish to customize and navigate to the Tags section.
  3. Create a new tag, and provide it with a name at the top (e.g., “Google Tag Configuration - example.com”).
  4. In the Tag Configuration panel, opt for the Google tag option.
  5. Proceed to configure your tag. Input your Google tag ID in the Tag ID field. Optional: You have the flexibility to set up additional configuration preferences to govern how your Google tag communicates with its designated destinations. If you’ve configured the Google tag and intend to retain all default settings, you can now proceed to Step 2: Create a Trigger.

Step 2: Create a trigger

Subsequently, configure a trigger that activates the Google tag when a user visits your website.

To prioritize the firing of the Google tag over other triggers, click on “Triggering” and select the “Initialization - All Pages” trigger. You can find additional information about Page triggers here.

  2. Input your website’s URL.
