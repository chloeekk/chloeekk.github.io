What is Google Ads Campaign?

Google Ads campaigns are an effective way to drive targeted traffic and leads to your website. These campaigns use a pay-per-click model, meaning that you pay only for the clicks on your ads that result in visitors coming to your website.

With Google Ads, you can create highly targeted campaigns for different audiences based on their search history, demographics, and other data. Google’s platform makes it easy to track the performance of your campaigns and make adjustments as needed. You can also optimize bids with automated tools to ensure that you’re getting a good return on investment (ROI).

Additionally, Google Ads campaigns provide valuable insights into user behavior such as which products or services they are interested in and how they interact with your website. By leveraging these insights, businesses can improve their overall marketing strategies and increase conversions.

What is Google Ads Campaign Goals?

Google Ads campaigns have various goals, such as increasing website traffic and leads, improving customer engagement, driving sales, and creating brand awareness.

With Google Ads, businesses can create highly targeted campaigns for different audiences based on their search history, demographics, and other data. These campaigns are used to maximize return on investment (ROI) by leveraging user behavior insights such as which products or services they are interested in and how they interact with your website.

Additionally, businesses can use Google Ads to test different marketing strategies and track the performance of each individual campaign allowing them to adjust their campaigns accordingly in order to achieve their desired goals.

Google Ads also allows businesses to run keyword-based campaigns or remarketing ads that target previous website visitors in order to reach a wider audience. Ultimately, it is up to the business owner or marketing team to decide which goals they want to prioritize when setting up a Google Ads campaign.

What is Google Ads Campaign Optimization Score?

Google Ads campaign optimization score is a metric that measures how effective an ad campaign is based on its performance. The score takes into account several factors such as reach, clicks, conversions and cost-per-click (CPC). It helps advertisers identify areas of improvement in their campaigns, such as if the reach or click rate are low. This allows them to make adjustments and strategies to maximize ROI and improve their overall performance.

What is Google Ads Campaign ID?

A Google Ads Campaign ID is a unique identifier assigned to each campaign created within Google Ads. It helps advertisers keep track of their campaigns and provides a way to organize and differentiate them from one another. The Campaign ID can be found in the top left corner of the campaign page in the Google Ads interface, and it usually follows the format of “###-#####-####”.

What is Campaign Name in Google Ads?

A Campaign Name in Google Ads is a user-defined name given to a specific campaign that helps advertisers differentiate between multiple campaigns and identify the purpose of each one. The Campaign Name is entered by the advertiser while creating a new campaign, and it can be edited at any time during the campaign’s lifespan. A well-defined Campaign Name can help advertisers quickly identify which campaign is performing well and which one needs improvement. It is also helpful when reviewing the performance of historical data.

Google Ads Campaign Types

  • Search Network campaigns: keyword-based ads that appear when someone searches for a certain term related to the product or service being advertised. This allows businesses to get in front of potential customers when they’re actively looking for something related to what the business offers.

  • Display Network campaigns: visual ads that appear on websites, videos, and apps across the internet, allowing businesses to gain exposure and reach new audiences by targeting users based on interests or demographics.

  • Video ad campaigns: a great way for businesses to reach out to their target audience as they allow them to create engaging video content which can be used for both brand awareness building and lead generation.

  • Shopping campaigns: enable businesses to advertise products on the Google search engine results page (SERP) allowing them to drive more traffic and conversions from prospective shoppers looking for specific items.

  • App campaigns: designed specifically for mobile app promotion and allow businesses to promote their applications across different channels such as YouTube, Gmail, and the Google Display Network

  • Performance Max Campaigns: allow marketers to optimize bids automatically in order improve the performance of their ad campaigns while controlling costs.

How to Create a Google Ads Campaign?

When establishing a Google Ads campaign structure, it is important to first define the desired goals and objectives of the campaign. For example, are you looking to increase website traffic or leads, improve customer engagement, drive sales conversions, or create brand awareness? Once these goals have been established, businesses can then select the type of campaign that will best help them achieve their desired objectives.

Here I will introduce the detailed steps to build a Google search ads campaign:

1 Create a Campaign

  1. Make sure you’re in campaign’s level and click “Add campaign”.

  2. Then you will need to complete the settings of the campaign you created.

  • Campaign name: set a good name for your campaign
  • Status: set “Enabled” or “Paused”. If you do not want to open this campaign after you upload it, you will need to choose “Paused”.
  • Budget: set a budget for this campaign
  • Budget type: “Average daily” or “Campaign total”
  • Bid strategy: there are mainly two kinds of bid strategies: manual bidding & automated bidding
  • Campaign type: there are various campaign types your can choose. In this guide, I will take “Search campaign” as an example.
  • Include search partners: if you enable this setting, your ads will be displayed not only in Google’s search result pages, but also in Google’s partners’ platforms, such as Youtube, Gmail, and etc.
  • Include Display Network: Expand display network will bring more traffic to you, but you also need to process the traffic may not related to your product.
  • Start date & End date: if you are creating a specific campaign for special events instead of a regular long-term campaign, you can set a valid period for it. Device-related settings (+ Devices, Desktop bid adjustment, Mobile bid adjustment, Tablet bid adjustment, TV screen bid adjustment): if your target audiences are mainly mobile device users, you can also set your campaign limited to mobile users. Also, you can set different bid for different devices.
  • Ad rotation: normally, Google Ads will display the best performing ads, if you hope every version of ads can be equally displayed, you can also choose other rotation options.
  • Language targeting: select the languages your target audiences may would like to use and it will help Google Ads to find them.
  • Location targeting: you also need to choose the country or religion your audiences may gather, or Google Ads will display your ads to all over the world.

2 Create a Ad group

After creating the campaign above, you need to move to “Ad groups” level and click “Add ad group”.

Here you also need to complete some settings related to ad group. Except the similar settings displayed in campaign, the most important part in ad group’s settings is your bid. Do you remember that you set a bid type when create your campaign. If you choose manual bid, you need to set default maximum cost-per-click. Or if you choose a automated bid, you may need to set “Target CPA”, which represents the price you would like to pay for each conversion.

3 Add keywords

Similarly, make sure you choose “Keywords” section, then click “Add keyword”.

You need to enter the keyword text and then choose its match type. There are three match types offered by Google Ads: Exact, Phrase, Broad. Please check you’v chosen the right types. It will be highly risk if you forgot to set it because the default settings of match type is broad match!

4 Add ads

The procedure to add ads is similar to the operations above. For search campaign will keywords, there are two kinds of ads: Responsive search ads and Expanded text ads:

  • Responsive search ads: let you create an ad that adapts to show more text — and more relevant messages — to your customers
  • Expanded text ads: similar to the text ads that you’re used to(please note that starting June 30, 2022, you’ll no longer be able to create or edit expanded text ads).

If you finish the four steps I introduced above and check all settings, you can click the “Post” button in the upper right corner of Editor to upload your first campaign. Let’s look forward to its performance.:)

Some Other Tips about Google Ads Campaigns

How to Cancel Google Ads Campaign?

To cancel a Google Ads campaign, follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Google Ads account.
  2. Go to the “Campaigns” tab in the left-hand navigation menu.
  3. Select the campaign you want to cancel.
  4. Click on the toggle switch next to the campaign name to pause it temporarily.
  5. Once paused, click on the campaign name to enter into the campaign settings.
  6. In the top right corner, click on the “Edit” button and then select “Remove”.
  7. Confirm the cancellation by clicking “Yes” on the prompt that appears.

Note that cancelling a campaign means the ads will no longer run, but any unpaid charges may still be billed to your account. You can also choose to delete the campaign instead of just pausing it if you do not plan to use it again in the future.

How to Turn Off Google Ads Campaign?

To turn off a Google Ads campaign, follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Google Ads account.
  2. Go to the “Campaigns” tab in the left-hand navigation menu.
  3. Select the campaign you want to turn off.
  4. Click on the toggle switch next to the campaign name to pause it temporarily. Once paused, the ads will no longer run, but any unpaid charges may still be billed to your account.

Alternatively, if you want to stop running your ads at a specific date and time, you can use the “End date” feature in Google Ads. To set an end date for your campaign, follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Google Ads account.
  2. Go to the “Campaigns” tab in the left-hand navigation menu.
  3. Select the campaign you want to edit.
  4. Click on “Settings” in the left-hand menu.
  5. Scroll down to “Schedule” and click on the pencil icon.
  6. Select “End date” and choose the date and time when you want your campaign to stop running. Click “Save”.

Once you save your changes, the campaign will automatically stop running at the specified end date and time.

How to Duplicate a Campaign in Google Ads?

To duplicate a campaign in Google Ads, follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Google Ads account.
  2. Go to the “Campaigns” tab in the left-hand navigation menu.
  3. Select the campaign you want to duplicate.
  4. Click on the “Edit” button.
  5. In the drop-down menu, select “Duplicate”.
  6. In the pop-up window that appears, enter a new name for the duplicated campaign, and select the settings you want to carry over from the original campaign.
  7. Click “Save”.

After creating a duplicate campaign, you can make any necessary changes to the new campaign, such as adjusting the budget, targeting, or ad creatives. Duplicating a campaign can save time and effort when creating multiple campaigns with similar settings or targeting. However, note that once a campaign is duplicated, it will have a new Campaign ID, so any reporting, tracking or automation linked to the original campaign will not apply to the new one.

How to Create Experiment Campaign in Google Ads?

To create an experimental campaign in Google Ads, follow these steps:

  1. Sign in to your Google Ads account.
  2. Go to the “Campaigns” tab in the left-hand navigation menu.
  3. Select the campaign you want to use as the basis for the experiment.
  4. Click on the “Drafts & Experiments” button in the top navigation menu.
  5. Click on the blue plus sign icon to create a new experiment.
  6. Choose “Create an experiment from an existing campaign” and select the campaign you want to use as a baseline.
  7. Give your experiment a name and set the experiment split (i.e., the percentage of traffic you want to send to the experiment).
  8. Make any desired changes to the campaign settings, such as adjusting the budget, targeting, or ad creatives.
  9. Click “Save and Apply” to launch the experimental campaign.

Once the experiment is live, you can monitor its performance against the original campaign within the “Drafts & Experiments” tab. Note that experimental campaigns run alongside the original campaign, so you will continue to be charged for both campaigns' clicks and impressions. It’s a good idea to let the experiment run for several weeks before making any significant decisions about its performance.

Why is My Google Ads Campaign Not Spending?

There could be several reasons why your Google Ads campaign is not spending:

  • Budget: It’s possible that you have exhausted your daily budget or set your budget too low to begin with. Check your campaign settings to make sure your budget is set high enough to allow for sufficient ad spend.

  • Bid: Your bid may be too low, and as a result, your ads are not showing up high enough in the search results. Consider raising your bid to see if your ads start receiving more impressions and clicks.

  • Ad Rank: Ad Rank is Google’s way of determining which ads to show and in what order. It’s influenced by factors such as your bid, ad quality, expected click-through rate (CTR), and landing page experience. If your Ad Rank is too low, your ads may not be appearing as often as they should be.

  • Targeting: You may have set overly restrictive targeting parameters, such as geographic location or time of day, which are limiting the potential reach of your ads. Review your targeting settings to ensure they are not too narrow.

  • Ad Status: If your ad is in “paused” status, it will not be eligible to serve. Ensure that all of your ads are enabled and approved for serving.

  • Ad Delivery: Google Ads uses an ad delivery system that determines which ads to serve at any given time based on a variety of factors. If your ad is not winning the auction for ad space, it may not be served as frequently as you’d like.

In summary, various factors can cause your Google Ads campaign not to spend. Review your campaign settings to ensure everything is correctly configured, adjust your bids and targeting as needed, and monitor your campaigns' performance to see if there are any improvements.
