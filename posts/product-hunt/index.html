What is Product Hunt?

Product Hunt is a platform that curates the best new products every day. It covers a wide range of categories, including the latest mobile apps, websites, and technology products. It’s a place where community members can submit, discuss, and upvote new products, making it a great resource for discovering innovative solutions and trends in tech.

In addition to being a discovery platform, it also serves as a launchpad for new ideas. Startups, designers, marketers, and other professionals often use Product Hunt to introduce their products to a wider audience and get feedback.

Product Hunt also has a mobile app available on Google Play, providing a front-row seat to everything happening in tech and startups, directly from your mobile device.

The Origin Story of Product Hunt

The origin story of Product Hunt dates back to 2013. Ryan Hoover, the founder, realized he was spending a lot of time talking about new tech products and gadgets with friends. This sparked an idea - a place where people could discover and discuss the latest products being built.

Originally, Product Hunt started as a simple email list that Hoover sent out to his friends. The email featured a curated list of interesting new products that he had come across. The list quickly gained popularity, and Hoover decided to turn it into a website.

To build the initial version of the website, Hoover partnered with Nathan Bashaw, a developer and product manager. They built the first version of Product Hunt in just one weekend. The original Product Hunt was hosted on a service called Linkydink.io, a tool for creating collaborative email newsletters.

After launching the website, the response was overwhelming. People loved the concept, and the user base grew rapidly. Startups began to see the value in launching their products on Product Hunt, and the platform became a crucial part of the tech ecosystem.

Product Hunt’s growth caught the attention of Y Combinator, a prestigious startup accelerator. Hoover and his team were accepted into the program, which helped them further develop and scale the platform.

In 2016, Product Hunt was acquired by AngelList, a platform for startups. However, it continues to operate independently, serving as a go-to resource for discovering the latest products in tech.

Navigating Product Hunt

Product Hunt is a dynamic and user-friendly platform designed to foster discovery, discussion, and promotion of new products. Here’s a more detailed breakdown of its key features and how you can navigate it effectively:

  • The Homepage: The homepage is the heart of Product Hunt’s activity. Here, you will see a list of new products posted for the day, ranked according to the number of upvotes they’ve received. The list starts with the ‘Product of the Day’ - the product that has garnered the most upvotes.

  • Product Cards: Each product is presented in a card-like format on the homepage. These cards provide a brief overview of the product, including its name, tagline, and the number of upvotes it has received. They also show a small preview image, which usually is a logo or screenshot related to the product.

  • Detailed View: Clicking on a product card will take you to a detailed view. Here, you’ll find a more extensive description of the product, links to the product’s website, and media content like images, videos, or GIFs. You’ll also see who hunted the product (i.e., who posted it on Product Hunt).

  • Upvoting: Upvoting is a central part of Product Hunt’s community engagement. If you like a product, you can click on the up arrow next to the product’s name to upvote it. This helps the product gain visibility and climb the ranks on the homepage.

  • Comments Section: Below the product details, there’s a comments section where makers and users can interact. Users can ask questions, share experiences, or suggest improvements, while makers can respond to feedback and provide additional information about their product.

  • Collections and Topics: Collections are handpicked groups of related products, curated by users. Topics, on the other hand, are broad categories like ‘Health’ or ‘Gaming’. Both of these features allow you to explore products based on your interests.

  • Submitting a Product: To submit a product, click on the ‘+’ icon located on the top right corner of the homepage. You’ll be asked to fill out a form with details about the product. After submission, the Product Hunt team will review it before it goes live.

  • User Profile: By creating a profile, you can engage more actively with the Product Hunt community. You can follow other users, see their activity, and get updates on the products they post, upvote, or comment on.

  • Notifications: The bell icon at the top right corner keeps you updated on your interactions and mentions.

  • Search Functionality: The search bar at the top allows you to search for specific products, collections, or people. You can filter the results based on relevance, popularity, or recency.

Remember, to fully participate in Product Hunt’s community (like upvoting or commenting), you need to create an account. You can sign up using your existing social media accounts or your email address.

Does Product Hunt cost money?

Product Hunt itself is free to use for users who want to discover and upvote products. Anyone can create a free account and begin exploring, upvoting, and commenting on new and innovative products.

However, if a company or individual wants to post a job listing on the Product Hunt Jobs portal, there is a fee. As per the information available, a single job listing costs $299 and the ad will be visible in the job directory for 30 days.

Moreover, Product Hunt offers a suite of tools called Ship, which helps makers and startups in building their products and connecting with early users. While there is a free tier for basic usage, more advanced features are part of the Pro and Super Pro tiers, which come at a monthly or annual cost.

Please note that while posting a product on Product Hunt is free, it’s subject to the platform’s rules and guidelines and requires approval by the Product Hunt team.

Case studies of successful startups that gained traction via Product Hunt

Product Hunt has been a launchpad for many successful startups. Here are a few case studies that highlight the impact of Product Hunt on these companies:

Robinhood

Robinhood, the commission-free investing app, gained significant traction after being featured on Product Hunt. On the day of its Product Hunt feature, Robinhood saw a 200% increase in sign-ups. The platform’s community provided valuable feedback that helped Robinhood refine its product offering.

Yo

Yo, an app that lets you send the word ‘Yo’ to your friends, was hunted on Product Hunt before it officially launched. The exposure led to thousands of downloads and significant media coverage. The app even reached #1 on the iOS App Store in the U.S., all within two days of being featured on Product Hunt.

Unsplash

Unsplash, a platform offering high-resolution photos for free, was another startup that benefitted enormously from Product Hunt. It went from 50,000 to 250,000 visitors in just a few months after being featured. Today, Unsplash is one of the leading platforms for high-quality stock images.

Slack

Although already gaining traction, Slack’s appearance on Product Hunt boosted its visibility within the tech community. It received hundreds of upvotes and positive comments, further propelling its growth.

Streak

Streak, a CRM built into Gmail, experienced a surge in traffic after being featured on Product Hunt. They received about 300% more sign-ups on their Product Hunt feature day compared to a typical day.

Predicting Trends with Product Hunt

Product Hunt is an excellent platform for predicting tech and startup trends due to its unique position in the ecosystem. It’s a hotspot for early adopters, tech enthusiasts, venture capitalists, and startup founders, which means it often showcases products that reflect emerging trends before they hit mainstream awareness.

Here are some ways you can use Product Hunt to predict trends:

  • Monitor High Ranking Products: The products that receive the most upvotes on Product Hunt often represent areas of high interest or emerging trends. By keeping an eye on these popular products, you can start to identify what’s resonating with the tech community.

  • Observe Recurring Themes: If you see similar types of products being posted regularly, it could indicate a rising trend. For instance, if there’s a sudden influx of AI-based chatbots or remote work tools, it’s likely these areas are trending.

  • Use Collections and Topics: Product Hunt’s Collections and Topics features allow you to see groups of related products. This can help you identify patterns and trends within specific categories.

  • Listen to the Community: The comments and discussions on Product Hunt can provide insight into what tech insiders and enthusiasts think about certain products and technologies. Their opinions can point towards upcoming trends.

  • Follow Influential Hunters: Some users on Product Hunt are particularly adept at spotting and hunting innovative products. Following these users can provide a valuable shortcut to identifying potential trends.

  • Analyze Yearly Roundups: At the end of each year, Product Hunt releases a roundup of the most upvoted products. Reviewing these can give you a summary of the year’s biggest trends and hint at what might continue to be popular in the future.

While Product Hunt can be a great resource for identifying trends, it’s not infallible. Trends can emerge and fade quickly, and just because something is popular on Product Hunt doesn’t guarantee its broader success. But by using the platform as one tool in your trend-spotting arsenal, you can gain valuable insights into the direction of the tech and startup world.

Learning from the Leaders: Insights from Top Product Hunt Users

Product Hunt has a vibrant community of tech enthusiasts, entrepreneurs, and investors. Some users have distinguished themselves through their active participation and knack for discovering innovative products. Let’s look at some insights gleaned from top Product Hunt users:

  • Ryan Hoover (Founder of Product Hunt): Ryan emphasizes the importance of building something people want. According to him, it’s crucial to understand your audience and create something that solves their problems or meets their needs. He also encourages makers to engage with their audiences actively, respond to feedback, and foster a sense of community around their products.

  • Kevin William David (Community at AngelList and Product Hunt): Kevin is known for his keen eye for unique and innovative products. His advice to product makers is to focus on differentiation - what sets your product apart from the competition? He also stresses the importance of a compelling product story to engage users and generate interest.

  • Chris Messina (Inventor of the hashtag, Product Hunter): Chris believes in the power of open-source and user-generated content. He emphasizes that giving users a sense of ownership can lead to a more engaged community. For product makers, he recommends focusing on transparency and openness to build trust with users.

  • Bram Kanstein (Creator of the viral product ‘Startup Stash’): Bram’s product, Startup Stash, became one of the most upvoted products ever on Product Hunt. His advice is to keep things simple and focus on usability. A product doesn’t have to be complex to be successful; it just needs to solve a problem effectively.

  • Nichole Elizabeth DeMeré (Community Growth at Zest.is): Nichole advocates for putting customers first. She believes that understanding your customers and their needs is the key to creating a successful product. She also emphasizes the importance of building a strong community around your product.

These insights from top Product Hunt users provide valuable lessons for anyone looking to launch a product or start a business. The common themes are understanding your audience, differentiating your product, engaging with your community, focusing on usability, and putting customers first.

Expanding Horizons: Product Hunt Beyond Technology

While Product Hunt is known for being a launchpad for tech startups and digital products, the platform extends beyond just technology. It’s a place where creators of all types can showcase their new products, gain visibility, and receive feedback from a community of early adopters and industry influencers.

Here are some examples of non-tech categories you can explore on Product Hunt:

  • Books: Authors and publishers often launch their books on Product Hunt. This includes not only e-books but also traditional print books across various genres like business, design, self-help, and more.

  • Podcasts: Many podcast creators use Product Hunt to gain new listeners. You’ll find podcasts covering a wide range of topics, from entrepreneurship to health, culture, and personal development.

  • Games: Both digital and physical games are launched on Product Hunt. This includes mobile games, console games, board games, card games, and more.

  • Food and Beverage Products: New food and drink products, including health foods, snacks, beverages, and even cooking appliances, are often featured.

  • Fashion and Apparel: Designers and fashion startups often launch their new collections or innovative clothing items on the platform.

  • Health and Fitness: This category includes fitness apps, workout equipment, health supplements, and wellness products.

  • Home and Living: Innovative home decor, furniture, kitchenware, gardening tools, and other home-related products are often hunted.

  • Education: Online courses, learning platforms, educational books, and learning tools for both kids and adults are regularly featured.

  • Art and Design: This includes everything from graphic design tools to art exhibits, photography, and creative projects.

Discussion of criticisms, controversies surrounding Product Hunt

Product Hunt has been a valuable platform for many startups and creators, but like any platform, it has faced some criticisms and controversies. Here are a few that have been brought up in various discussions:

  • Lack of Transparency: Some users have expressed concerns about the lack of transparency in the product selection and ranking process. They’ve argued that it’s unclear how and why certain products are featured on the homepage while others are not.

  • Biased Towards Certain Products: There are claims that Product Hunt tends to favor certain types of products, particularly those related to tech and startups, which can be limiting for creators in other fields.

  • Influence of Popular Hunters: Some critics argue that the success of a product on Product Hunt can depend heavily on who submits it. Products hunted by well-known or influential members of the community may receive more attention, regardless of the product’s inherent quality or innovation.

  • Impact on Server Loads: For smaller startups, the sudden influx of traffic from being featured on Product Hunt can lead to server crashes and other technical issues, which can negatively impact their launch day.

  • Limited Long-Term Impact: While being featured on Product Hunt can lead to a significant spike in traffic and user sign-ups, some critics argue that this boost is often short-lived and doesn’t necessarily translate into long-term growth.

Predictions for the platform’s future development and growth

Predicting the future development and growth of a platform like Product Hunt involves some speculation, but based on its current trajectory and the broader tech and startup ecosystem, here are a few possibilities:

  • Expansion into New Categories: As we’ve seen, Product Hunt isn’t limited to tech products. We could see further expansion into other categories, such as art, fashion, music, and more, as the platform seeks to broaden its user base and appeal.

  • Enhanced Community Features: Product Hunt’s community is one of its greatest assets. The platform may introduce new features to enhance community interaction, such as improved commenting systems, direct messaging, or user groups based on interests.

  • Deeper Integration with AngelList: Since Product Hunt was acquired by AngelList, there’s potential for deeper integration between the two platforms. This could include features that make it easier for startups launched on Product Hunt to find investors on AngelList, or vice versa.

  • More Tools for Makers: Product Hunt has already introduced tools like Ship to help makers build and launch their products. We might see the introduction of more such tools or services, perhaps focusing on areas like product development, marketing, user feedback, or analytics.

  • International Growth: While Product Hunt already has a global user base, there’s potential for further international growth. This could involve introducing localized versions of the platform, partnerships with international startup communities, or features tailored to specific regions.

  1. Increased Monetization Efforts: As with any business, Product Hunt will likely continue exploring ways to monetize its platform. This could involve introducing new paid features, expanding its job board, offering premium subscriptions, or partnering with brands for sponsored content.

Remember, these are just predictions and the actual future of Product Hunt may unfold differently. However, given its position at the heart of the startup ecosystem, it’s likely that Product Hunt will continue to evolve and grow in exciting ways.
