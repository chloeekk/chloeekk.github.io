What is PPC?

Pay-per-click, or PPC, is an internet advertising model used to drive traffic to websites. In this model, an advertiser pays a fee each time one of their ads is clicked by a user. This form of online marketing allows businesses to appear on the search engine result page (SERP) when certain keywords related to their business are searched. PPC can be applied across various digital platforms, including search engines like Google and social media networks. It’s a way for advertisers to essentially buy visits to their site, rather than attempting to earn those visits organically.

PPC marketing, also known as search engine marketing (SEM), enables advertisers to bid on the perceived value of a click in relation to the keywords, platforms, and audience type they want to target. When done correctly, the pay-per-click payment model can be profitable for both the ad publisher and the advertiser.

Why is a Competitor Analysis for PPC Important?

Competitor analysis is a crucial component of any successful PPC (Pay-Per-Click) strategy. By understanding your competitors' actions, you can inform your own strategies and make data-driven decisions that can lead to improved performance in your PPC campaigns. Here’s why competitor analysis for PPC is so important:

  • Discovering Industry Trends: Competitor analysis offers a bird’s eye view of the industry. It helps to identify common practices, popular keywords, and trending ad styles. This information can help you stay current and competitive.

  • Uncovering Effective Keywords: By analyzing your competitors' keyword strategy, you can discover which ones are driving traffic to their sites. This can help you refine your own keyword list, adding those that are effective and removing those that aren’t.

  • Gaining Ad Copy Insights: Reviewing your competitors' ad copy can provide inspiration for your own. You can learn what language, calls to action, or value propositions resonate with your target audience, and test these insights in your own ads.

  • Informing Budget Decisions: Understanding your competitors' PPC spend can provide valuable context for your own budgeting decisions. If they’re outspending you, it might be necessary to increase your budget to compete effectively. On the other hand, if they’re spending less but achieving better results, it could indicate a need for more efficient campaign management on your part.

  • Identifying Market Gaps: Competitor analysis could reveal gaps in the market that your competitors have overlooked. These gaps could be unique selling propositions, less competitive keywords, or underserved customer segments. Filling these gaps could give your business a competitive edge.

  • Setting Benchmarks: Finally, competitor analysis provides benchmarks against which you can measure your own PPC performance. This can help you set realistic goals and expectations, track your progress, and adjust your strategy as needed.

In short, a thorough competitor analysis for PPC can provide actionable insights that enhance your campaigns' effectiveness. By understanding your competitors' strategies, you can make informed decisions that improve your PPC performance, ultimately leading to increased traffic, leads, and sales.

WHO are Your Competitors?

Your competitors are other businesses or individuals who offer a similar product or service as yours to the same target market. They can be categorized into two main groups:

  • Direct Competitors: These are businesses that sell a product or service that is essentially the same or very similar to yours, and they are targeting the same customer base. For example, if you own a pizza restaurant, your direct competitors would be other pizza restaurants in your area.

  • Indirect Competitors: These are businesses that offer different products or services but fulfill the same need or solve the same problem for your target customers. Using the previous example, an indirect competitor to your pizza restaurant might be a burger joint or a sushi restaurant. While they don’t sell pizza, they’re still competing for the same dining-out dollars.

It’s essential to identify both types of competitors when doing a competitor analysis. Direct competitors will give you insight into what strategies are being used by businesses most similar to yours, while indirect competitors can provide a broader view of the overall market and help you identify unique strategies or niches that may not have been tapped into yet.

Steps to Effective PPC Competitor Research

Effective PPC competitor research requires a thoughtful and structured approach. Here are some detailed steps to guide you through the process:

Identify Your Competitors

Start by identifying who your competitors are. Use keyword research tools to find out who else is bidding on the same keywords as you are. You can also manually search for your primary keywords in Google and see which companies appear in the paid ads. Don’t forget to consider both direct competitors (those offering similar products/services) and indirect competitors (those targeting the same audience but with different products/services).

Analyze Competitors' Keywords

Once you’ve identified your competitors, use tools like SEMRush, SpyFu, or Ahrefs to analyze the keywords they’re targeting. Look at:

  • Broad keywords: These are general terms related to your industry.

  • Long-tail keywords: These are more specific phrases that customers might use.

  • Negative keywords: These are terms your competitors are actively excluding from their campaigns.

Investigate Ad Copy and Creative Elements

Examine the language, tone, and creative elements used in your competitors' ads. Take note of:

  • The unique selling propositions (USPs) they highlight.

  • The calls-to-action (CTAs) they use.

  • The emotional triggers they aim to hit.

  • The overall design and aesthetic of their ads.

Examine Their Landing Pages

Click through some of your competitors' ads to analyze their landing pages. Consider:

  • The design and layout of the page.

  • The messaging and how it correlates with the ad copy.

  • The CTAs on the page and how they’re positioned.

  • The overall user experience, including load times and mobile optimization.

Understand Their Offers and Pricing

Look at what your competitors are promoting in their ads. Are they offering a discount, a free trial, free shipping, or a bonus with purchase? Also, look at their pricing strategy and how it compares to yours.

Use Tools to Monitor Competitor Activity

Use PPC monitoring tools to keep track of your competitors' activities over time. This will help you spot any changes in their strategy, such as new keywords or shifts in ad spend.

Analyze Their Performance

Use available data to evaluate your competitors' performance. This could include their ad rank, click-through rate, and conversion rate. While exact data might not be accessible, third-party tools can often provide estimates.

Apply Your Findings

The final and most crucial step is to apply what you’ve learned to your own PPC campaigns. This could mean bidding on new keywords, adjusting your ad copy, redesigning your landing pages, or rethinking your offers.

Top Competitor Analysis for PPC Tools

There are numerous tools available that can help you conduct a thorough competitor analysis for your PPC campaigns. Here are some of the top tools:

SEMRush

SEMRush is a comprehensive digital marketing tool that offers a wealth of information about your competitors' online activities. It provides insights into their organic and paid search strategies, social media presence, and content marketing efforts. The tool allows you to see which keywords your competitors are bidding on, their ad copy, and even their estimated ad spend. It also provides historical data, which can help you identify trends and patterns in your competitors' PPC strategies.

SpyFu

SpyFu is a competitive intelligence tool that allows you to spy on your competitors' search marketing efforts. It shows every keyword that your competitors have bought on Google Ads, every organic rank, and every ad variation in the last 14 years. This eliminates the guesswork of PPC and helps you understand what works for your competition. The tool also provides contact information for leads and backlink data.

Ahrefs

Ahrefs is primarily known for its robust backlink analysis, but it also offers a PPC analysis feature. You can use it to see the exact keywords your competitors are ranking for in paid search, how much traffic these keywords bring, and the value of this traffic. It also provides detailed backlink data, so you can see which sites are linking to your competitors.

iSpionage

iSpionage is a competitive intelligence tool that allows you to take a deep dive into your competitors' PPC strategies. You can see the keywords they’re targeting, their ad copy, and even their landing pages. It provides useful metrics like average ad position, monthly budget, and more. The tool also offers SEO competitive research and keyword monitoring features.

Adbeat

Adbeat specializes in providing insights into your competitors' display advertising strategies. It can show you where your competitors are placing their ads, what kind of creative they’re using, and how their strategies evolve over time. The tool covers 120+ ad networks, including Google Display Network and Outbrain.

Moz Keyword Explorer

While not specifically a PPC tool, Moz’s Keyword Explorer can help you understand which keywords your competitors are likely targeting in their PPC campaigns based on their organic rankings. The tool provides keyword suggestions, SERP analysis, and even lets you see how difficult it would be to rank for a particular keyword.

Case Study

A great real-world example of how PPC competitor research has helped a business succeed can be seen in the case of AdEspresso, a digital advertising company.

AdEspresso wanted to increase their visibility and conversions through PPC advertising, but they were operating in a highly competitive market. They began by conducting thorough competitor research to understand the strategies used by successful companies in their industry.

Using various tools, they analyzed the keywords their competitors were bidding on, their ad copy, and their landing pages. This gave them insights into what was working for other companies and helped them identify gaps in their own strategy.

Based on their findings, AdEspresso made several changes to their PPC campaigns. They started bidding on new keywords, tweaked their ad copy to make it more compelling, and redesigned their landing pages to improve user experience and conversion rates.

As a result of their competitor research and subsequent changes, AdEspresso saw a significant increase in both traffic and conversions. Their cost per acquisition (CPA) decreased, while their return on ad spend (ROAS) increased.

This example illustrates the power of PPC competitor research. By understanding what their competitors were doing, AdEspresso was able to refine their own strategy and achieve better results.

Conclusion

Conducting thorough PPC competitor research is a critical component of any successful digital marketing strategy. By using tools like SEMRush, SpyFu, Ahrefs, iSpionage, Adbeat, and Moz Keyword Explorer, businesses can gain valuable insights into their competitors' PPC strategies, including the keywords they’re bidding on, their ad copy, and their landing pages.

This information allows businesses to identify gaps in their own strategies, discover new opportunities, and make data-driven decisions that can lead to increased visibility, more conversions, and ultimately, higher returns on ad spend. As seen in the real-world example of AdEspresso, effective competitor research can significantly enhance a company’s PPC performance.

However, it’s essential to remember that competitor research is not a one-time activity. The digital landscape is continually evolving, with competitors adjusting their strategies and new trends emerging. Therefore, businesses must regularly conduct PPC competitor research to stay ahead of the curve and ensure their marketing efforts remain effective.

At the end of the day, the goal of PPC competitor research is not to mimic your competitors but to learn from them. It’s about finding what works, understanding why it works, and applying those insights to your own unique strategy.
