How to Use Google Analytics 4 Audiences?

When delving into Google Analytics 4, users often encounter features such as segments, audiences, and comparisons. This can be a source of confusion for those new to GA4, as these features, while similar, have distinct differences.

This blog post will focus on the concept of audiences in Google Analytics 4, explaining their nature and the scenarios in which they prove to be beneficial.

What are audiences in Google Analytics 4?

Audiences refer to specific groups or subsets of users that are not only useful for analytical purposes but can also be targeted for advertising. These key groups, vital to your business, can be formed using dimensions, metrics, and events (including sequences of events).

As Google Analytics continually gathers data on new users, these audiences are regularly reassessed to ensure they align with the set criteria.

Where can you use audiences in Google Analytics 4?

Used in Google Ads

Audiences are instrumental in displaying your advertisements. By linking your GA4 property with a Google Ads account and activating Google Signals, these audiences become accessible in the Audience Manager of Google Ads. This allows you to employ them as targeting choices in your paid advertising campaigns.

Used for comparison

Audiences also prove valuable in the context of comparisons. In Google Analytics 4, every standard report features an “Add comparison” option at the top. This function enables you to contrast different user groups based on specific criteria, one of which includes the audience name.

How to create an Audience in Google Analytics 4?

In Google Analytics 4, audiences can be created in two primary ways: either through segments within Exploration reports or by navigating to Configure > Audiences.

Create an audience from segments

In GA4, segments are exclusively accessible in explorations. To access them, navigate to the Explore section on the left side of the GA4 interface and select an option like Blank, Free form, Funnel exploration, or similar.

Within the first column under ‘Variables,’ you’ll find the Segments section. To create a new segment, click the plus icon. Alternatively, if you wish to use an existing segment as an audience, click the three dots next to that segment and select ‘Edit’.

When you opt to edit an existing segment, you’ll be taken straight to the segment builder. In this interface, on the right side, you’ll find a checkbox labeled ‘Build an audience.’ Click on this checkbox to unveil more options.

You have the ability to determine the duration a user remains part of the audience once they meet the specified criteria, with a minimum of one day and a maximum of 540 days.

Upon saving the segment, the audience is automatically formed and begins to gather data. However, it’s important to note certain limitations:

  • Audiences start collecting data only from the point of their creation; they do not work retroactively.
  • You cannot make changes to audiences, except for modifying their name and description. You also have the option to set up the audience trigger, provided it hasn’t been configured already.
  • On the audience builder’s right side, a summary or preview of the audience size is displayed. It’s important to remember that these figures are merely estimates, based on data from the past 30 days, particularly in the context of audience numbers.

Create an audience from Audiences

In the Google Analytics 4 interface, navigate to Admin > Audiences from the left sidebar. There, you can choose from pre-suggested templates or opt to Create a custom audience.

The layout in this section is very similar to what you encountered in the Segments area. It allows you to add condition groups, sequences, and exclusion groups.

Audience triggers

Google Analytics 4 introduces a novel feature called “Audience triggers” which differs from Universal Analytics (GA3). This feature allows GA4 to automatically generate an event when a visitor joins an audience, and you can designate this event as a conversion.

For instance, to track the generate_lead event as a conversion only once per session (since GA4 typically tracks conversions each time they occur), you should:

  1. Create an audience that includes visitors who complete a specific sequence in the same session: starting with a session_start event, followed by a generate_lead event. The sequence must begin with session_start to ensure it’s limited to “once per session,” as this event occurs only once per session.

  2. Set up this audience to trigger an audience event, named, for example, au_generate_lead_once_per_session (where “au” stands for “audience”). You can choose any name. This trigger should be configured to activate every time a user re-enters the audience.

  3. In the Google Analytics 4 interface, register au_generate_lead_once_per_session as a conversion event.

Another scenario:

If your e-commerce store tracks select_promotion events (for banner clicks) and purchase events (both part of the e-commerce setup), you can:

  1. Form an audience for visitors who perform these two events in sequence within the same session.

  2. Create an audience trigger that generates an event each time a visitor re-enters the audience. Name this event something like “au_purchase_after_promoclick”.

This setup allows you to easily monitor a report detailing how often visitors click on internal promotions and subsequently make a purchase.

Edit, duplicate or delete an audience

After creating an audience in Google Analytics 4, your options for editing it are quite restricted. You can only:

  • Change its name
  • Modify its description
  • Add an audience trigger, but only if it wasn’t added previously

It’s important to note that audiences in GA4 are not retroactive; they only begin to accumulate data from the time they are created. Consequently, GA4 doesn’t permit altering their conditions. This lack of retroactivity is a limitation, as it would be beneficial if members of an audience could be re-evaluated if the conditions were changed.

To edit basic information about an audience, navigate to Admin > Audiences. Click the three dots next to the audience you wish to modify and select Edit.

In the audience/segment builder, you’ll have the option to rename the audience, edit its description, or add an audience trigger.

Additional actions include duplicating the audience and archiving it, the latter being the equivalent of deleting an audience.

It’s crucial to understand that duplicating an audience and modifying its settings is not an effective workaround for the inability to edit existing audiences. Since audiences are not retroactive, creating a new audience by duplicating and adjusting one will mean it starts gathering data only from the moment of its creation, without any historical data.
