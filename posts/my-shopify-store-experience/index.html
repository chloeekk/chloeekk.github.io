The beginning of the story

Finally made up my mind to leave my job in the middle of 2022 for a change of scenery. After determining the entry time of the next company, thinking of trying to find another possible way back for myself, I thought of opening an independent website for cross-border e-commerce. Compared with Amazon, which has complicated procedures and high audit threshold, Shopify is obviously more suitable for me. A week’s time to run through the business registration, just before the PayPal account, and do not have to register separately. But in fact there are many, many more things to do.

Preparation before the official opening of the site

Brand story

Obviously I was in a rush and didn’t have a good idea of what I wanted to sell, let alone a novel and persuasive branding concept. I urgently looked for some independent station and cross-border e-commerce experience sharing, and finally patted my head and decided - or I’ll sell tablecloths, it looks like the market for home furnishings is quite big, the unit price is okay, and the transportation is safe.

Then I started to look at a lot of overseas home furnishing brands' websites, imitating each other’s slogans, brand stories, shipping terms, refund policies and so on.

Logistics provider

Logistics is also an area I know nothing about. I also looked at a few cross-border logistics companies that were highly recommended in the industry, and then signed a contract with Yuntu. Then the other party sent me a huge excel sheet full of quotes for various types of shipments and to different regions outside the country. Standard express, general cargo, with electricity, registered …… are all words I can’t understand. Some regions also require additional taxes.

Selection

I actually didn’t know what kind of goods overseas users like. So I went back to Amazon to look at the style of the more popular items, and then went to the suppliers to find similar products. I was worried that the quality of the goods would not be good and would bring the risk of returning them, so I even bought one specifically to check the quality first.

Costing

You can’t just decide on the price of a product or you will end up losing money. When you really start to calculate the cost, you will find that the shipping cost is much more expensive than the purchase price of the product itself, and you also have to add the handling fee of the collection platform. At the end of the day, in order to try to be consistent with the average price on the market, will find that there is actually not much room for profit.

Finally, we can open the site.

Uploading products

When I can finally upload products to the store, I already regret a little bit. Because tablecloths come in many sizes, as few as three and as many as a dozen. I also need to consider the conversion of inches and centimeters so that some users won’t be able to read the specifications of the products.

Product images are also a big pain in the ass. Each product has to have at least three pictures, and they have to be from different angles. The image names need to be changed manually after uploading to make sure they are SEO friendly.

The most troublesome part is to write the product description, including the material of the product, pattern style, measurements, washing and caring suggestions, FAQs and so on.

It’s hard to finish editing in the backend, and when you look at the page, you realize that the text is not in the right place, and you have to adjust it again.

Tax Returns

After I opened my independent website, I realized that I have to declare my taxes every quarter, even if the store has no income. Once I forgot to declare the last quarter’s tax and got fined.

Access to Google tools

The access to Google Search Console and Google Analytics is not too smooth. Although there are a lot of detailed tutorials on the internet, I still encountered some problems in actual operation. You need to go to the template provided by Shopify to find the corresponding position to insert Google’s code.

Logistics Testing

For the sake of logistics shipping steps and time, I contacted a KOL on Instagram and gifted the person two items, provided she shared her experience after receiving the items.

Add another payment method

I was worried that some users would give up paying because they didn’t or couldn’t use PayPal, so I applied for 2checkout as an alternative payment method.

Stuck without traffic

After the products were uploaded my store was half dead for a long time, maybe a few exposures a day. I didn’t want to spend extra money on running ads, so I planned to find traffic from blog posts and common social media platforms.

Writing blog posts

I don’t know much about tablecloths and I can’t write any professional articles, so I can only read a lot of articles written by other people in search engines and think about what I can write if I am going to recommend my own products. In addition to posting it on my own website, I also synchronize it on Medium.

With little success. It wasn’t until I had a post about table skirting later in the day that suddenly brought tons of exposure that I finally got a little bit of traffic to my store.

Instagram, Pinterest, Twitter, Facebook Operations

I started uploading some pictures of items on Instagram with different tags, hoping to attract some interested people, but unfortunately not with links. I received a few likes, but very few comments. One or two people have asked about the merchandise via private message, but that also went away because I didn’t reply in time.

In the meantime, I’ll go along and post the image on Pinterest, Twitter, and Facebook, which are three platforms that available with links to merchandise.

Quora Operations

I started answering a lot of questions on Quora, hoping to attract users to my site by sharing some useful tips.

Tiktok Operations

I also opened an account on Tiktok and posted some short videos from time to time, which basically had only two hundred or so views except for the first item.

Paid traffic

Can’t wait or choose to pay for traffic.

Ran a couple of campaigns on top of Instagram first for a hundred bucks each with likes and comments, but no one bought them. Can only be used to get an idea of what type of items people probably prefer.

Then I opened another Google Ads account and tried Performance Max, Display, and Search ads, unfortunately no conversions.

I gave up.

After operating an independent site off and on for more than a year, I finally chose to shut down the site. The final revenue was $35.78.The cost of Shopify operations, the difficulty of expanding traffic, and the problem of shipping physical products …… all made me feel that there was no point in spending any more time. But what I must also admit is that I didn’t put the maximum effort into this thing. A lot of things were just done, but not taken seriously.

It was a valuable experience, too.
