Research Phase of Content Creation

Before you begin writing, you must complete your research. This phase helps you determine your article’s potential impact and prevents you from wasting time on content with no ranking opportunity.

1. Interpreting Search Intent

Search intent determines whether you can rank.

  • Evaluate ranking potential: Look at the SERP. If the top results are all product pages, the keyword has strong transactional intent—no matter how good your informational article is, it will struggle to rank.
  • Identify true competitors: Compare only with websites that share the same content purpose. If you’re writing a professional guide but the top results include short Quora answers, ignore those and analyze other high-quality guides.
  • Build trust: The fastest way to establish authority is to meet the user’s exact needs. Give users exactly what they came for, not what you think is good for them.

e.g. Before writing, search your target keyword. If the top 10 results are all videos, consider producing a video instead of an article.

2. Keyword Evaluation and Research

Don’t be misled by “high search volume.” You need to assess a keyword’s true value.

  • Traffic quality: Traffic does not equal conversions. Choose keywords that attract visitors who can actually become leads—not just clicks.
  • Determine content format: Check the SERP to understand the type of content users expect. Are they looking for an “ultimate guide,” or just a short definition?
  • Build a content outline: Prioritize Google’s People also ask and Related searches. These are real user concerns—turn them directly into your H2 or H3 headings.
  • Analyze competitor structure: Review the top five pages. Note word count, format (listicle, tutorial, FAQ), and the core questions they answer.

Good keyword research should naturally produce an outline full of real user questions—not just a keyword list.

3. Content Strategy Based on Awareness Stages

Your content should match the user’s level of awareness about the problem and the solution.

Awareness StageUser MindsetYour Content Task
UnawareDoesn’t know they have a problem.Describe the current situation and highlight risks/opportunities.
Problem-AwareKnows the pain point but not the solutions.Explain the cause of the problem and introduce possible solutions.
Solution-AwareKnows solutions exist but unsure which to choose.Compare available options and present your solution.
Product-AwareKnows your product and compares alternatives.Provide product reviews, case studies, or detailed comparison charts.
Most AwareReady to buy and just needs a push.Provide a clear CTA, purchase link, or limited-time offer.

Never explain basics to a “most aware” user. They’re ready—give them the buy button.

Four Core Types of SEO Tools

1. Keyword Research Tools

Identify and quantify search demand.

  • Purpose: Show monthly search volume, keyword difficulty, and long-tail suggestions. They help you confirm whether users want information or are ready to buy.
  • Value: Ensures your content aligns with market demand and prevents resources from being wasted on keywords with no search intent or extremely high competition.
  • Main tools: Ahrefs, SEMrush, Google Keyword Planner.

Prioritize long-tail queries with clear intent—they usually convert far better than broad high-volume terms.

Four Types of Search Intent

TypeUser GoalRecommended Content Format
InformationalWants to learn somethingGuides, tutorials, explainers, videos
NavigationalWants a specific site/pageBrand homepage, login page
CommercialComparing optionsReviews, top-10 lists, comparison tables
TransactionalReady to buyProduct pages, signup pages, discount pages

2. Rank Tracking Tools

Monitor your keyword positions in the SERP.

  • Purpose: Track daily position changes and compare historical trends. Gives you a clear view of how your pages perform in search over time.
  • Value: A direct feedback loop for SEO effectiveness. If rankings drop, you can take action before traffic declines.
  • Main tools: AccuRanker, SE Ranking, Google Search Console (average position data).

Focus on the distribution trend of rankings (e.g., number of keywords in top 3/top 10), not just single-keyword fluctuations.

3. Site Audit Tools

Automatically detect technical issues affecting crawling, indexing, and user experience.

  • Purpose: Scan and identify 404 errors, duplicate content, missing Meta tags, oversized images, and Robots.txt issues.
  • Value: Large sites can’t manually check every technical component. Audit tools highlight issues that block search engine crawlers.
  • Main tools: Screaming Frog, Sitebulb, Ahrefs Site Audit.

Fix issues by priority: critical indexing blockers first (Errors), then UX-related warnings.

4. Backlink Analysis Tools

Track your backlink profile and domain authority.

  • Purpose: Identify which external sites (and their authority) link to you. Also analyze competitors’ backlink sources for link-building opportunities.
  • Value: Backlinks remain a core ranking signal. Monitoring backlinks helps you measure authority growth and detect harmful spam links.
  • Main tools: Ahrefs, Majestic, SEMrush.

Focus on “Referring Domains.” A single link from multiple different high-quality domains outweighs many links from the same site.

SEO Content Quality Standards

E-E-A-T: Key Dimensions of Content Value

E-E-A-T guides Google’s assessment of content quality. It’s not a single algorithm, but it sets the quality ceiling for your content.

  • Experience: Demonstrates first-hand involvement. Use real photos, personal stories, and insights from actual practice.
  • Expertise: Shows knowledge depth through data citations, industry credentials, or a professional author bio.
  • Authoritativeness: Reflects industry recognition, often at the domain level—e.g., backlinks from reputable sites.
  • Trustworthiness: The most critical element. Requires accuracy, transparency, HTTPS encryption, clear contact information, and honest, non-misleading titles.

In YMYL fields (finance, health), Trustworthiness carries enormous weight—any misinformation can destroy rankings.

From Topical Coverage to Topical Authority

SEO aims to make your site the “expert” in a topic. This requires alignment at both the page and site level.

Topical Coverage — Page Level

  • Definition: Ensures a single page covers all important details users might want about the topic.
  • Execution: A guide on hiking boots should include brand picks, waterproof tests, grip evaluations, sizing tips, etc.
  • Goal: Become the most comprehensive and helpful resource for that keyword.

Topical Authority — Site Level

  • Definition: Build domain-wide authority by publishing high-quality content across a focused niche.
  • Execution: Use the topic cluster model—create multiple related articles and interlink them.
  • Goal: Help search engines recognize your site as a complete knowledge hub within the topic, boosting rankings across the cluster.

Authority takes time. Focus on one niche first—dominate it, then expand.

Internal Linking: Building On-Site Authority Flow

Internal links connect your pages and guide both users and search engines.

1. Anchor Text Optimization Principles

Anchor text is a key signal that helps search engines understand the linked page.

  • Use descriptive text: Avoid “click here.” Use meaningful text like “ergonomic chair buying guide.”
  • Keep it natural and varied: Don’t repeat the same anchor for the same link. Mix branded terms, exact-match keywords (“SEO tools”), and partial-match phrases.

2. Three Steps for Effective Internal Linking

Step 1: Build Topic Clusters Interlink related content within the same topic. This helps search engines see the semantic relationship between pages.

Step 2: Direct Link Equity Flow (Link Juice) Identify high-authority pages and link from them to your target “money pages” to boost ranking strength.

Step 3: Add Contextual Links Place links naturally within the narrative whenever a concept is mentioned. This improves both SEO and user engagement.

How to Optimize Technical SEO

1. Crawling & Indexing Control

Ensure search engines can access your site smoothly and discover all important pages.

  • XML Sitemap: Provide a complete list of essential URLs to help crawlers locate them quickly.
  • Robots.txt: Define which directories (such as admin pages) should be blocked from crawlers.
  • Practical tip: Always use a plain-text editor when editing Robots.txt to avoid formatting issues that may break instructions.

2. Site Architecture & URL Structure

A solid structure helps both crawlers and users navigate your site more efficiently.

  • Flat architecture: Ensure users and crawlers can reach any page from the homepage within three clicks.
  • URL best practices: Keep URLs clean, all lowercase, and free of special characters.

3. Page Speed & Performance

Load speed is a direct ranking factor and has a significant impact on user experience.

  • Image optimization: Use WebP instead of PNG/JPG and compress images without sacrificing visual quality.
  • Lazy Loading: Load images and videos only when they enter the viewport to reduce initial load time.
  • Code minimization: Compress CSS and JavaScript files and remove redundant code.

4. Mobile Optimization

Since Google uses mobile-first indexing, mobile performance is critical.

  • Responsive design: Ensure layouts adapt automatically to various screen sizes.
  • Usability checks: Verify button sizes are easy to tap and text remains readable without zooming.

5. Duplicate Content & Error Handling

Prevent search engines from wasting crawl budget on duplicated or irrelevant pages.

  • Canonical tags: When similar content exists across multiple URLs, use Rel=Canonical to specify the primary version.
  • Redirect strategy: For permanently removed pages (404 errors), use 301 redirects to the most relevant active page to preserve ranking value.

6. Structured Data

Help search engines better understand the meaning and context of your content.

  • Rich results: Deploy JSON-LD to enable enhanced SERP features such as star ratings, prices, or FAQs.
  • Practical tip: After implementing structured data, always validate it using Google’s Rich Results Test.

Advanced SEO Techniques

Structured Writing & Keyword Placement

Great content balances human readability with algorithmic clarity.

  • Four core keyword placements: Include your primary keyword in the meta title (preferably near the front), H1 tag, URL, and body/H2 tags (ideal for long-tail keywords).
  • Semantic relevance (Entities): Naturally incorporate related concepts. For example, an article about “fitness plans” should also discuss “diet guidance” and “training frequency.”
  • Clear hierarchy: Use H2–H4 headings with predictive titles that help readers locate answers instantly.
  • Readability optimization: Keep paragraphs and sentences short, and prioritize mobile reading experience.
  • Multimedia integration: Images and videos should clarify complex concepts—not merely decorate the page.

Humanizing AI-Generated Content

Google does not reject AI content—only unhelpful content. You must refine AI output manually:

  • Add E-E-A-T elements: Include personal experience, unique insights, or proprietary data to elevate authority.
  • Fact-check: Manually verify numbers, statistics, and external links supplied by AI.
  • Style refinement: Remove clichés (like “in today’s digital landscape”), vary sentence rhythm, and emulate genuine human cadence.

Optimizing Existing Content

You don’t always need new content—updating current assets often delivers faster results.

  • Refresh evergreen content: Regularly review high-traffic articles and update outdated data, replace broken links, and maintain relevance.
  • Strengthen internal linking: Strategically direct authority from strong pages to ranking targets.
  • Deploy structured data: Use JSON-LD to earn rich results such as stars, FAQs, or product details.
  • Google Discover optimization: Use high-quality images with a minimum width of 1200px for timely or news-driven topics.

Content Repurposing

Maximize the reach of every content asset:

  • Format transformations:

    • Convert high-performing videos into detailed blog articles.
    • Condense data-heavy blogs into sharable infographics.
    • Break long articles into social media micro-posts.
    • Rewrite top-performing content for publication on industry media as guest posts to acquire high-authority backlinks.

From SEO to AI Visibility

The Shift in Logic: Ranking vs. Mention

AI search is not a replacement for SEO—it is an evolution.

  • Traditional SEO goal: Win top search rankings and attract clicks.
  • AI optimization goal: Get your brand or content included directly in AI-generated answers.
  • Connection: LLMs rely heavily on publicly available web content. High-ranking pages are more likely to be referenced by AI models.

SEO helps you rank; AI optimization helps you get mentioned. Both rely on trust, authority, and accurate answers.

Helping AI Recognize & Retain Your Brand

AI must know you exist before it can cite you. That requires becoming a widely recognized entity.

  • Cross-web consistency: Maintain consistent branding—names, product terms, and key personnel—across your website, social platforms, review portals (G2, Trustpilot), and business listings (LinkedIn, Crunchbase).
  • Off-site presence: AI reads far beyond your blog. It learns from Wikipedia, Reddit, Quora, industry publications, and aggregator platforms.

Create numerous high-quality external mentions so AI strongly associates your brand with specific problems and solutions.

Building AI Trust & Authority Signals

AI prefers content that appears reliable.

On-site signals: Demonstrate depth & experience

  • Robust author profiles: Go beyond job titles—list concrete credentials (e.g., “10 years of SaaS experience”).
  • First-hand material: Use case studies, proprietary datasets, and project breakdowns to showcase real “Experience.”
  • Citations: Support claims with reputable external sources to strengthen credibility.

Off-site signals: Establish social consensus

  • High-quality endorsements: Backlinks from authoritative sites, mentions in industry media, or even simple brand references.
  • Third-party reviews: Verified user feedback on platforms like G2.

Reverse-engineer the pattern: Ask an AI model what it cites for a topic. Study those pages. Then create something better.

Content quality: Keep it human

  • Natural language flow: AI favors clear, logical, conversational writing.
  • Avoid robotic tone: Even with AI assistance, inject unique brand voice, specific examples, and human emotional perspective.

How to Make Content AI-Friendly

Structural Optimization: Designing for AI Retrieval

AI does not look at your page design—it only reads the textual logic within your HTML. You must organize your content as if you were structuring an archive:

  • Question-based subheadings: Use “What are the benefits of [Product Name]?” instead of vague titles like “Product Benefits.” Users phrase queries this way when asking ChatGPT or Gemini, and this structure directly aligns with search intent.
  • Lead with the answer: Immediately provide a clear conclusion in the first sentence beneath each question-based heading. AI often extracts this concise statement as the answer summary.
  • Modular structuring: Each key idea should stand alone in its own section. Rely heavily on bullet points and tables—LLMs love table structures because the data is clean, explicit, and easy to quote accurately.
  • Leverage FAQ blocks: One of the most effective ways to capture long-tail queries. AI frequently copies the FAQ Q&A sections verbatim.

Technical Foundation: Ensuring AI Can “See” Your Content

If AI crawlers cannot access your site, your content—no matter how good—won’t appear in results.

  • Check Robots.txt: Ensure you haven’t accidentally blocked LLM-related crawlers (like GPTBot). If these crawlers are blocked, your brand will never appear in AI responses.

  • HTML is the core: Make sure essential content is written directly in HTML, not hidden behind JavaScript, pop-ups, or “click to load” interactions. AI does not click like a human user.

  • Submit your sitemap: Particularly to Bing (since ChatGPT relies heavily on Bing’s index).

  • Alt text for images: Helps AI interpret visual and multimedia content.

  • Structured data (Schema): A set of “hidden tags” designed specifically for machines:

    • Organization Schema: Defines brand identity.
    • Article Schema: Labels titles, dates, and authors to improve credibility.
    • Person Schema: Links your content to real contributors, strengthening E-E-A-T.
    • Product Schema: Highlights price, reviews, and availability.

Entity Reinforcement: Helping AI Build Brand Associations

AI is not only reading your pages—it is constructing relationships between “entities.”

  • Schema removes ambiguity: Schema tells machines “This article was written by Jane Smith”; entity optimization ensures the entire internet knows who Jane Smith is.
  • Occupy third-party nodes: Make sure your brand appears on Wikidata, Crunchbase, LinkedIn, and vertical industry directories. These databases are foundational sources for AI knowledge graphs.
  • Leverage UGC (User-Generated Content): AI strongly trusts platforms like Reddit, Quora, G2, and Trustpilot because these conversations reflect authentic human dialogue.
  • Videos & podcasts: Provide accurate transcripts—AI is ingesting massive amounts of YouTube and podcast scripts.
  • Community presence: Mentions of your brand in Reddit threads or YouTube comments significantly increase your chances of being cited by AI.

Establish Niche Dominance

Build Topical Authority That AI Cannot Ignore

LLMs no longer reference standalone articles—they prefer domains demonstrating complete topical mastery.

  • Expand from points to systems: Traditional SEO might write “10 best payroll software.” To build authority, you must cover the full ecosystem: tax compliance, contractor classification, regional policy differences, HR integrations, and more.

  • Own the complete answer: AI Overviews (like Google AIO) often pull multiple sections from the same domain. If you cover every angle comprehensively, you can “dominate” an entire AI-generated answer.

  • Avoid fragmented authority:

    • No thin content: Don’t publish scattered, shallow short posts.
    • Stay topic-focused: Don’t write about sales one week, team-building the next, and fundraising the week after. Dig deeply into one cluster until you become the definitive source.
    • Add supporting pieces: Don’t only create “ultimate guides.” AI prefers how-to tutorials, comparisons, objection handling, and specific case breakdowns.

Use PR to Strengthen AI Visibility

AI search reflects collective consensus. It prioritizes brands endorsed by authoritative, high-trust media.

  • Why PR matters for AI: Mentions in reputable publications (like TechCrunch or industry-leading media) are picked up by AI—even without backlinks—and treated as authority signals.

  • High-value PR assets:

    • Original research & data reports: Publish proprietary data-driven industry insights. These unique assets are highly quotable and often become primary citation sources for AI.
    • Expert commentary & guest contributions: Publishing thought leadership on authoritative sites significantly increases your chances of being cited when AI answers expert-level queries.
    • Rankings & podcasts: Get your product included in “Top 10” lists. AI also crawls podcast transcripts, making mentions in shows a strong entity signal.

The Key Metric: From CTR to “Mention Rate”

In the age of AI-generated search, many user questions are answered directly on the SERP. With fewer clicks happening, we must measure visibility through Mention Rate.

Why “Mentions” Are the New North Star Metric

  • Indicator: When users ask industry questions, seek recommendations, or compare products, does AI include your brand in its recommendation list?
  • Value conversion: Even without clicks, appearing in AI responses builds trust and mental availability. Brands frequently mentioned by AI enjoy significantly higher final-stage conversion rates.

The Underlying Synergy Between SEO and AI

  • Rankings remain foundational: LLMs do not invent content—they heavily rely on search engine indexes like Google’s.
  • Causal link: Declines in traditional rankings reduce the material available for AI training. Without traditional SEO, Mention Rate has no source to draw from.

How to Track & Monitor Mention Rate

You need a systematic measurement framework to ensure your brand stays within AI’s field of view.

  • Manual stress tests:

    • Select 20 Top Prompts that reflect your core value proposition (e.g., “What is the best payroll software for startups?”).
    • Regularly test them across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews.
    • Log how often and how prominently your brand appears.

  • Professional monitoring tools:

    • Share of Voice (SOV): Use emerging visibility tools such as Ahrefs Brand Radar or OmniSEO to automate monitoring of your brand’s share of mentions across major LLMs.
    • Attribution analysis: Track which external pages AI cites. If your competitors are being quoted, analyze their content structure and build a strategy to outperform them.
