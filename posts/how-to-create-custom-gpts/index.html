How to Create Custom GPTs

OpenAI, the innovator of ChatGPT, has introduced a novel approach that allows individuals to develop their personalized ChatGPT variants. These are referred to as GPTs, and they can be tailored to align with your business or personal objectives, with the process taking just a few minutes.

Follow these steps to construct a bespoke ChatGPT through the GPT builder offered by OpenAI.

What are GPTs?

GPTs are tailored editions of ChatGPT, fashioned by users of OpenAI. Simply convey to the GPT builder in clear English your desired creation, and the builder will handle the rest.

Additionally, you have the option to program your GPT to navigate the internet, create visuals using DALLE·3, and execute programming scripts.

While ChatGPT Plus or Enterprise subscribers can already tailor ChatGPT outputs with specific directives, the GPT builder elevates customization in two significant ways:

  1. Construct numerous custom GPTs. You’re not restricted in the number of GPTs you can create, allowing for seamless switching among various chatbots to obtain AI-driven assistance suitable for different situations. In contrast, custom instructions restrict you to a single instruction set per account.

  2. Import knowledge data files. Rather than manually inputting text from each reference you want ChatGPT to use in its responses, you can directly upload these knowledge documents to your GPT builder. The builder will then seamlessly integrate this information.

How to build your own custom ChatGPT

1. Sign in to Your OpenAI Account

To begin, ensure you have a ChatGPT Plus or Enterprise account. If you already possess the necessary account, simply click on ‘Log in’ to initiate your chatting experience.

2. Initiate Your GPT

  1. Navigate to the sidebar and select ‘Explore’. Following this, click on ‘Create a GPT’.

The GPT builder presents a dual-view interface: on one side, the ‘Create’ panel is where you input prompts to construct your chatbot; on the other, the ‘Preview’ panel enables real-time interaction with your chatbot during its development, simplifying the process of refining and tweaking it.

  1. Type your directives into the message box on the Create page and hit Enter or Return.

  2. Following your input, the GPT builder will offer several suggestions based on your instructions, including a name for the chatbot, a profile image, and some initial conversation starters.

You have the option to either go with the initial recommendations or request the GPT builder to alter them. Should you choose to accept the initial suggestions, remember that you can modify them at a later stage.

  1. The GPT builder will ask for more detailed instructions from you to precisely adjust the behavior of your chatbot.

Don’t worry if you’re uncertain about how to tweak your chatbot’s behavior. My recommendation is to experiment with your chatbot in the Preview panel—engage with it as you typically would—and let its responses guide your adjustments. For instance, if your chatbot delivers extensive answers, you could instruct the builder to keep its responses brief. Alternatively, if the chatbot presents facts without references, you might direct it to consistently cite its sources.

  1. Persist in fine-tuning your prompts until your chatbot begins producing the types of responses you envision for your final chatbot.

3. Personalize Your GPT

Having established the fundamental aspects, delve into further personalizing your GPT with more sophisticated options.

  1. Select ‘Configure’.

  1. Should you choose to, it’s possible to alter your chatbot’s name and description in the usual manner. For changing other advanced settings, here’s the procedure:
  • Profile picture. Click on the profile picture icon. You have the option to upload a custom photo or utilize DALLE·3 to auto-generate a new one. To guide what type of image DALLE·3 should produce, click on ‘Create’ and input your specific instructions.

  • Instructions. Modify the instructions provided by the GPT builder, or input extra directives or rules outlining how your chatbot should or should not operate.

  • Conversation starters. To delete a prompt, simply click the ‘X’ next to it. Alternatively, you can add a new prompt in any vacant ‘Conversation starters’ field.

  • Knowledge. Do you wish for your chatbot to use your company’s style guide as a basis for crafting its responses? Or perhaps you’d like it to review PDFs of customer personas for extra context. If so, click on ‘Upload files’ and incorporate any pertinent files for it to consult.

  • Capabilities. Your chatbot is set up to automatically navigate the web and generate AI-created images. If you desire it to execute code or perform data analysis as well, simply select ‘Code Interpreter’.

  • Actions. Should you require your chatbot to access information from external sources or perform tasks beyond the ChatGPT platform, opt for ‘Add Actions’. This option allows you to define the manner in which your chatbot should utilize any third-party APIs.

4. Store Your GPT

  1. After you’re satisfied with your chatbot, hit ‘Save’. (In case you’re updating an already created custom GPT, choose ‘Update’ instead.)

  2. Decide on the audience for your custom chatbot: choose from ‘Only me’, ‘Only people with a link’, or ‘Public’. For Enterprise plan users, there’s an additional choice of ‘Anyone at [your company]’.

  1. Distribute Your GPT For those who have set their custom GPT to be accessible by others, here is a straightforward method to share your chatbot.

  2. Go to the sidebar and select ‘Explore’.

  3. Choose the GPT you wish to share.

  4. Click on the downward arrow (∨) located beside your chatbot’s name.

  5. Select ‘Copy link’.
